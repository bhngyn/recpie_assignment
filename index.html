Thịt kho

A classic Vietnamese comfort food

Thịt kho tau, Vietnamese braised pork belly with eggs is an everyday dish for Vietnamese families everywhere. During Tết, it's traditionally eaten on the first day of the new year. Braising the pork low and slow in a mixture of coconut soda, fish sauce and aromatics yields a luxurious and rich dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs Pork Belly
  • 8 hard-boiled eggs
  • 12 fl oz coconut soda (1 can)
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 yellow onion
  • 4 tbsp thick soy sauce (nuoc mau)
  • 4 garlic cloves minced
  • 1 shallot
  • 3 cups water
  • 2.5 tbsp granulated sugar

Instructions

  1. Cut the pork into 1.5" cubes.
  2. Bring 2 quarts of water to a roaring boil. Parboil the pork for 1 to 2 minutes. Drain the pork and rinse it under running water
  3. Marinate the parboiled pork in 2 tbsp garlic, half a shallot, thick sauce, sugar and fish sauce.
  4. In a small pot add vegetable oil. Saute remaining garlic, shallots until fragrant.
  5. Add marinated pork with all its liquid. Toss gently
  6. Add the coconut sod and water to the pot. The pork should be just covered by the liquid.
  7. Bring the pot to a boil, then lower to a simmer. Let the pork simmer for 1 and a half to 2 hours. Check and stir every 20 minutes.
  8. Make hard boiled eggs.
  9. Peel the hard boiled eggs. Fry the hardboiled eggs in a pan. Set aside.
  10. Add the hardboiled eggs in the last 30 to 40 minutes of cooking.
  11. Reduce the cooking liquid to about 1/3 of the original quantity.
